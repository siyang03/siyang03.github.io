Research Experience

  • In 2023 Summer, I participated Professor Raúl Briceño's (rbriceno@berkeley.edu) research team at UC Berkeley, studying the role of three-body system in quantum computations of scattering observables. My project is to study the relativistic 3-body scattering amplitude in 1 dimension and to seek trimers under the φb threshold. Under Raúl's guidance, I derived the integral equation of the scattering amplitude above the φb threshold, and solved it by programming in Python. I then read Raúl's previous paper on the relativistic 3-body scattering amplitudes (arXiv:2303.04394 [nucl-th]), learned the numerical methods introduced in it, and applied them to my program. Finally, I extended the solution below the φb threshold to study whether bound states exist, equivalently speaking, to seek for poles of the relativistic 3-body scattering amplitude below the φb threshold. I carefully analyzed the singularities and branch cuts of the exchange propagator and the 2-body scattering amplitude on the momentum complex plane. I found that the Efimov effect does not exist in 1-dim, and found a new bound state (trimer) irrelevant to the Efimov effect.
